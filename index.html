The Problem

Motherhood shouldn't feel overwhelming

The weeks after birth are sacred — yet so many Nigerian mothers navigate them without the reliable support they deserve. Whether you're a first-time mum or growing your family, the gaps are real.

  • 🔍

    No reliable help after delivery

    Finding trustworthy postpartum support in Nigeria is time-consuming, informal, and often unreliable — with no standard vetting process.

  • 👩‍👧

    Family support isn't always available

    Modern life means grandmothers and relatives are often far away or unavailable. The traditional omugwo support system has broken down for many families.

  • 📋

    Hiring help feels risky and expensive

    Without a trusted platform, hiring a caregiver means navigating unverified referrals and hoping for the best — at a critical, vulnerable time.

  • 🍼

    New mothers carry too much alone

    Sleep deprivation, breastfeeding challenges, postpartum mood changes — without proper support, recovery becomes a struggle instead of a season of joy.

Contact Us

Omugwo is a marketplace platform that connects new mothers and families in Nigeria with vetted, trained postpartum caregivers. Drawing from the traditional Igbo practice of "omugwo" — where family comes to support a new mother — we modernize and formalize that care for today's families.

All Omugwo caregivers undergo background checks, identity verification, health screenings, and complete our Omugwo Academy training programme. We assess cultural competency, newborn care skills, and emotional intelligence before any caregiver is listed on our platform.
